In 1957, Dennis Lindley published "A Statistical Paradox" in Biometrika, showing that frequentist and Bayesian methods can reach opposite conclusions as sample size approaches infinity. This became known as the Jeffreys-Lindley Paradox.

The LCC Paradox (Lovric-Claude-ChatGPT) reveals something arguably more devastating:

For any statistically significant result (p < 0.05), two Bayesians using different "reasonable" prior scales will reach opposite conclusions ‚Äî one saying "reject H‚ÇÄ" while the other says "accept H‚ÇÄ."

Unlike the Jeffreys-Lindley paradox (which requires n ‚Üí ‚àû), the LCC Paradox occurs with realistic sample sizes (n = 20, 50, 100) and shows that Bayesians cannot agree among themselves.
